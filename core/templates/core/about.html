The person behind the studio

Built and led by someone who understands the distance between raw data and a decision.

I am the founder of Mimalodi Visual Data Space. My work sits at the intersection of data analysis, business communication, and visual design — turning messy reporting into something leadership teams can trust and act on.

Before building this studio, I spent time working across analytics, reporting, and data presentation in environments where the quality of insight directly shaped business outcomes. That experience taught me that most reporting problems are not technical — they are communication problems. Teams have data, but they struggle to frame it in a way that lands with stakeholders.

That is the gap I focus on. I help teams move from scattered metrics to structured, presentation-ready reporting systems — dashboards that support weekly reviews, visual narratives that hold up in board rooms, and KPI frameworks that make performance conversations calmer and more productive.

Working principles
  • Clarity over complexity. If the audience cannot understand it without a walkthrough, it is not ready.
  • Structure before style. I define what needs to be communicated before designing how it looks.
  • Business language first. Reports and dashboards are written for decision-makers, not analysts.
  • Presentation-grade output. Everything is built to be shared with confidence in professional settings.
